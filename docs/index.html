Why this matters

Rare earth feedstock is valuable, fragmented, and strategically under-coordinated.

Supply is dispersed across scrapyards, e-waste streams, dismantlers, ITAD firms, industrial processors, and OEM reverse logistics programs. Commercially relevant material often lacks standardized characterization, reliable price discovery, and direct access to specialized buyers.

01

Fragmented supply

Recyclers struggle to source consistent volumes of rare-earth-bearing feedstock across regions and categories.

02

Opaque quality

Material specs, composition estimates, and form factors are inconsistently captured, slowing procurement.

03

Weak price discovery

Scrappers often lack transparent market signals or access to specialist processors willing to bid.

04

Critical mineral pressure

Circular critical mineral supply is becoming central to industrial resilience, electrification, and magnet demand growth.

High-value feedstock From embedded magnets inside motors to manufacturing fines and HDD assemblies.

Each category is presented with the commercial and technical detail recyclers need to evaluate quality, handling complexity, and sourcing relevance.

NdFeB magnets

NdFeB magnet scrap

High-value magnet feedstock from motors, HDDs, speakers, and manufacturing offcuts.

Typical source: traction motors, HDDs, wind systems Form: loose magnets, bonded assemblies, swarf Quality note: coating, contamination, mixed grades
SmCo

SmCo magnet scrap

Lower-volume but strategically important feedstock for specialty high-temperature applications.

Typical source: aerospace, sensors, industrial equipment Form: manufacturing scrap, broken magnets Quality note: brittle fractions and mixed assemblies
Motors

Electric and traction motors

Whole motors, dismantled fractions, or shredded streams with embedded rare-earth-bearing magnets.

Typical source: EVs, industrial robotics, appliances Form: whole motor, rotor, stator, shredded fraction Quality note: magnet access and copper crossover matter
HDDs

Hard disk drive magnet assemblies

ITAD and e-waste derived material with strong repeatability for specialist buyers.

Typical source: ITAD firms, e-waste aggregators Form: assemblies, depopulated units, mixed fractions Quality note: plating, adhesives, mixed stream handling
MRI / Industrial

MRI and industrial equipment magnets

Large-format assets with project-based removal, handling, and buyer coordination needs.

Typical source: hospitals, salvage, industrial operators Form: assemblies, systems, decommissioned equipment Quality note: extraction complexity and logistics readiness
Fines / Sludge

Powders, fines, and manufacturing residues

Intermediate materials for processors with assay and batch validation requirements.

Typical source: magnet manufacturing and polishing Form: sludge, dust, fines, captured particulate Quality note: moisture, contamination, assay variance

Strategic importance

Rare earth recycling matters because supply resilience matters.

Electrification, wind generation, robotics, electronics, and defense demand are pushing magnet and rare earth supply into sharper focus. Circular sourcing reduces dependence on virgin extraction and strengthens secondary material access for industrial users.
